# Workplace Violence Liability: W-S States Guide

**States Covered:** South Dakota, Utah, Washington, Wyoming
**Last Updated:** May 11, 2026

---

## South Dakota

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No specific statewide workplace violence law
- Minimal state-level regulation
- Relies on federal standards
- Focus on tribal sovereignty issues

**Key Citations:**
- South Dakota Codified Laws § 36-31B et seq. (South Dakota Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- South Dakota Administrative Rules
- OSHA General Duty Clause

**Industry Focus:** Healthcare, agriculture, federal/tribal facilities

---

## Utah

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No comprehensive statewide law
- Healthcare facilities regulated under Utah OSHA
- Some local requirements in Salt Lake City
- Focus on emergency response planning

**Key Citations:**
- Utah Code Ann. § 34A-6-101 et seq. (Utah Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- Utah Admin. Code R614-1 (Utah OSHA Standards)
- Salt Lake City Municipal Code

**Industry Focus:** Healthcare, education, mining

---

## Washington

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No specific statewide workplace violence prevention law
- Relies on federal OSHA standards and General Duty Clause
- Healthcare facilities may have additional requirements under WAC 296-62
- Some local jurisdictions (Seattle, Tacoma) have specific ordinances

**Key Citations:**
- RCW 49.17 (Washington Industrial Safety and Health Act)
- WAC 296-800 (Employee Emergency Plans and Fire Prevention Plans)
- OSHA General Duty Clause applies

**Industry Focus:** Healthcare, retail, social services

---

## Wyoming

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No specific statewide workplace violence prevention law
- Relies heavily on federal OSHA standards
- Limited state enforcement capacity
- Focus on high-risk industries

**Key Citations:**
- Wyoming Statutes § 27-11-101 et seq. (Wyoming Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- Wyoming Administrative Code
- OSHA General Duty Clause

**Industry Focus:** Energy, agriculture, tourism
