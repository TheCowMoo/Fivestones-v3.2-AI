# Workplace Violence Liability: N-M States Guide

**States Covered:** Montana, Nevada, New Mexico
**Last Updated:** May 11, 2026

---

## Montana

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No comprehensive statewide law
- Healthcare facilities have basic requirements
- Focus on rural and remote workplace safety
- Limited regulatory oversight

**Key Citations:**
- Montana Code Annotated § 39-71-101 et seq. (Montana Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- ARM 24.29 (Montana OSHA Standards)
- OSHA General Duty Clause

**Industry Focus:** Healthcare, forestry, mining

---

## Nevada

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No specific statewide workplace violence law
- Healthcare facilities have some requirements
- Las Vegas metropolitan area has local ordinances
- Focus on casino/gaming industry security requirements

**Key Citations:**
- NRS 618.440 (Nevada Occupational Safety and Health)
- Clark County ordinances
- OSHA General Duty Clause

**Industry Focus:** Gaming, hospitality, healthcare

---

## New Mexico

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- **Limited state law** - No comprehensive workplace violence prevention statute
- Healthcare facilities subject to federal requirements
- Tribal lands complicate jurisdiction
- Focus on mining and energy sector safety

**Key Citations:**
- New Mexico Statutes Annotated § 50-9-1 et seq. (New Mexico Occupational Health and Safety Act)
- New Mexico Administrative Code Title 11, Chapter 1
- 29 CFR 1910.38 (Emergency Action Plans)
- 29 CFR 1910.165 (Employee Alarm Systems)

**Industry Focus:** Mining, oil/gas, healthcare, federal/tribal facilities

**Case Law Considerations:**
- *Zamora v. St. Vincent Hospital* (2018) - Established duty of care for workplace violence prevention
- *Bustos v. United States* (tribal jurisdiction issues)

**Tribal Land Considerations:**
- Navajo Nation has separate workplace safety regulations
- Pueblo lands may have tribal-specific requirements
- Federal facilities on tribal lands subject to additional oversight

**Mining Sector Requirements:**
- MSHA regulations apply to mining operations
- Enhanced emergency response requirements
- Remote location challenges for emergency services

**Oil and Gas Industry:**
- BSEE and PHMSA regulations for offshore operations
- Pipeline safety requirements
- Worker transportation safety considerations
