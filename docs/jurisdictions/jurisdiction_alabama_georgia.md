# Workplace Violence Liability: Western States Guide

**States Covered:** Arizona, California, Colorado, Idaho, Montana, Nevada, New Mexico, Oregon, South Dakota, Utah, Washington, Wyoming
**Last Updated:** May 10, 2026

---

## Federal Baseline

All US employers are subject to OSHA's General Duty Clause (Section 5(a)(1)) requiring employers to provide a workplace free from recognized hazards, including workplace violence. This serves as the foundation for all state-specific requirements.

---

## Arizona

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No comprehensive statewide law
- Healthcare facilities regulated under Arizona OSHA
- Phoenix metropolitan area has local requirements
- Focus on emergency preparedness

**Key Citations:**
- Arizona Revised Statutes § 23-401 et seq. (Arizona Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- Phoenix City Code
- OSHA General Duty Clause

**Industry Focus:** Healthcare, education, retail

---

## California

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- **SB 553 (2024)**: Comprehensive Workplace Violence Prevention Plans
- Required for healthcare facilities (hospitals, clinics, long-term care)
- Must include: Risk assessments, prevention plans, training, reporting procedures
- Effective July 1, 2024 for covered facilities
- Civil penalties up to $25,000 per violation

**Key Citations:**
- California Labor Code § 6401.9
- California Code of Regulations Title 8 § 3342
- SB 553 (Chapter 875, Statutes of 2023)

**Industry Focus:** Healthcare, emergency services, social services, retail

## Washington

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No specific statewide workplace violence prevention law

**Key Citations:**

- OSHA General Duty Clause applies
