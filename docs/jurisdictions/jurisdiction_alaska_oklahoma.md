# Workplace Violence Liability: Alaska-Hawaii-Oklahoma Guide

**States Covered:** Alaska, Hawaii, Oklahoma
**Last Updated:** May 10, 2026

---

## Alaska

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No comprehensive statewide workplace violence law
- Remote location challenges for emergency response
- Focus on oil/gas industry and federal facilities
- Limited state enforcement in rural areas

**Key Citations:**
- Alaska Statutes § 18.60.010 et seq. (Alaska Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- 8 AAC 61 (Alaska OSHA Standards)
- OSHA General Duty Clause

**Industry Focus:** Oil/gas, fishing, federal facilities

---

## Hawaii

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No specific statewide workplace violence law
- Island geography affects emergency response
- Focus on tourism and healthcare sectors
- Some local ordinances in Honolulu

**Key Citations:**
- Hawaii Revised Statutes § 396-1 et seq. (Hawaii Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- Hawaii Administrative Rules § 12-46
- Honolulu Municipal Code

**Industry Focus:** Tourism, healthcare, agriculture

---

## Oklahoma

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No comprehensive statewide workplace violence law
- Healthcare facilities have some requirements
- Focus on energy sector safety
- Limited state-level enforcement

**Key Citations:**
- Oklahoma Statutes Title 40 § 401 et seq. (Oklahoma Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- Oklahoma Administrative Code 380:1-1 et seq.
- OSHA General Duty Clause

**Industry Focus:** Energy, healthcare, manufacturing</content>
