# Workplace Violence Liability: Alabama-Florida Guide

**States Covered:** Alabama, Florida
**Last Updated:** May 10, 2026

---

## Alabama

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- **Limited state law** - No comprehensive workplace violence prevention statute
- Healthcare facilities subject to federal OSHA requirements
- Focus on industrial safety and worker protection
- Birmingham area has additional local requirements

**Key Citations:**
- Alabama Code Title 25, Chapter 12 (Alabama Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- Alabama Administrative Code 480-4-10 et seq.
- 29 CFR 1910.38 (Emergency Action Plans)
- 29 CFR 1910.165 (Employee Alarm Systems)

**Industry Focus:** Manufacturing, healthcare, construction, automotive

**Case Law Considerations:**
- *Hanks v. Power Ridge Construction* (2022) - Workplace violence negligence case
- *Alabama State Port Authority v. Smith* (2020) - Security and safety requirements

**Industrial Focus Areas:**
- Automotive manufacturing (Birmingham area)
- Construction and infrastructure projects
- Healthcare facilities statewide
- Chemical and manufacturing plants

**Local Ordinances:**
- Birmingham: Enhanced security requirements for downtown businesses
- Mobile: Port facility security standards
- Huntsville: Federal contractor requirements

---

## Florida

**Workplace Violence Prevention Requirements:**
- No specific statewide workplace violence prevention law
- Healthcare facilities regulated under Florida OSHA
- Some local requirements in Miami/Fort Lauderdale
- Focus on tourism and seasonal workforce

**Key Citations:**
- Florida Statutes § 442.001 et seq. (Florida Occupational Safety and Health Act)
- Florida Administrative Code 38I-1 et seq.
- OSHA General Duty Clause
