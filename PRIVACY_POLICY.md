## 1. Introduction

Pursuit Pathways Inc. ("**Pursuit Pathways**," "**we**," "**our**," or "**us**") is a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business in Bradenton, Florida. We provide workplace safety solutions including onsite threat assessments, eLearning programs, in-person training, train-the-trainer certification, and a cloud-based Safety Assessment Platform (the "**Platform**") accessible at www.pursuitpathways.com.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information when you visit our website, use our Platform, engage our professional services, or interact with us in any other way. By using any of our services, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

We are committed to handling personal information responsibly, transparently, and in accordance with applicable United States federal and state privacy laws.

## 2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information in two ways: information you provide directly to us, and information collected automatically through your use of our services.

### 2.1 Information You Provide Directly

**Account and profile information.** When you or your organisation creates an account on the Platform, we collect your name, email address, job title, and organisational affiliation. Organisation administrators may also provide their organisation's name, address, and contact details.

**Assessment and audit data.** When using the Platform to conduct facility safety assessments, we collect the responses, notes, photographs, floor plans, and other documentation you enter or upload during the assessment process. This data is associated with the specific facility and organisation account under which it is submitted.

**Incident reports.** Our Platform allows employees to submit anonymous workplace incident reports. These reports may contain descriptions of incidents, location information, and optional contact information if the reporter chooses to provide it. We do not require reporters to identify themselves, and we do not log IP addresses in connection with anonymous incident submissions.

**Training and eLearning records.** When you participate in our eLearning programs or in-person training, we collect completion records, assessment scores, and certification information.

**Communications.** When you contact us by email, phone, or through our website, we retain records of those communications including your name, contact details, and the content of your message.

**Payment information.** When you purchase services, we collect billing information. Card payments are processed by Stripe, Inc., a PCI-DSS-compliant payment processor. We do not store full card numbers on our systems. For direct bank transfers (ACH or wire), we collect the information necessary to process the transaction.

### 2.2 Information Collected Automatically

**Usage data.** We collect information about how you interact with the Platform, including pages visited, features used, session duration, and actions taken. This information is used to improve the Platform and is not sold to third parties.

**Device and technical information.** We collect standard technical information including browser type, operating system, and general geographic region (derived from IP address). We do not build individual profiles from this data.

**Cookies and session tokens.** We use session cookies to maintain your authenticated state while you are logged in. These cookies are set as `httpOnly` and `secure` and are not accessible to third-party scripts. We do not use advertising or tracking cookies.

## 4. How We Share Your Information

We share personal information only in the following limited circumstances.

**Within your organisation.** Assessment data, audit reports, and incident reports submitted through the Platform are accessible to designated administrators within your organisation's account. Incident reports submitted anonymously are visible only to organisation administrators with the `org_admin` role — they are not visible to other members of your organisation.

**Service providers.** We share information with third-party vendors who assist us in operating the Platform and delivering our services. These vendors are contractually required to use your information only as directed by us and to maintain appropriate security measures. Our primary service providers include our cloud hosting provider, database provider, payment processor (Stripe, Inc.), and AI/LLM service provider used to generate assessment recommendations.

**Business transfers.** If Pursuit Pathways is involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets, personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will provide notice before personal information is transferred and becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

**Legal requirements.** We may disclose personal information if required to do so by law, court order, or governmental authority, or if we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, protect your safety or the safety of others, or investigate fraud.

**With your consent.** We may share personal information for any other purpose with your explicit consent.

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, to maintain your account, to comply with our legal obligations, and to resolve disputes.

## 6. Data Security

We implement technical and organisational measures designed to protect your personal information against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. These measures include encrypted data transmission (TLS/HTTPS), session authentication with signed cookies, role-based access controls, rate limiting on all API endpoints, and security headers (Content Security Policy, HTTP Strict Transport Security, X-Frame-Options) on all web responses.

No method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. In the event of a data breach that affects your personal information, we will notify you as required by applicable law.

## 8. Anonymous Incident Reporting

Our Platform includes a feature that allows employees to submit workplace incident reports anonymously. We have designed this feature with privacy as a priority:

- Reporters are not required to create an account or provide their name.
- We do not log the IP address of anonymous incident submissions.
- Reports are visible only to designated organisation administrators (`org_admin` role) within the specific organisation to which the report was submitted.
- Reporters receive a unique tracking token that allows them to check the status of their report without revealing their identity.
