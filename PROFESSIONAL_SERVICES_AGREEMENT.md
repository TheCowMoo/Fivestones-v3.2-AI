## 1. Agreement Overview

This Professional Services Agreement (this "**Agreement**") governs the provision of professional services by **Pursuit Pathways Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business in Bradenton, Florida ("**Pursuit Pathways**," "**we**," "**our**," or "**us**"), to the client identified in the applicable Statement of Work ("**Client**").

Pursuit Pathways provides workplace safety solutions including onsite facility threat assessments, eLearning program delivery, in-person safety training, and train-the-trainer certification programs (collectively, "**Professional Services**"). Each engagement is governed by this Agreement together with a mutually executed **Statement of Work** ("**SOW**") that specifies the scope, deliverables, timeline, and fees for that engagement.

By executing a Statement of Work that references this Agreement, the Client agrees to be bound by the terms herein.

## 3. Scope of Services

### 3.1 Statements of Work

All Professional Services will be performed pursuant to a mutually executed SOW. Each SOW will specify: (a) a description of the services to be performed; (b) the deliverables to be produced; (c) the project timeline and key milestones; (d) the fees and payment schedule; (e) the names or roles of key Pursuit Pathways personnel assigned to the engagement (if applicable); and (f) any Client responsibilities or prerequisites.

### 3.2 Changes to Scope

Any changes to the scope of services described in a SOW must be agreed upon in writing by both parties through a change order. Pursuit Pathways reserves the right to adjust fees and timelines to reflect scope changes.

### 3.3 Client Cooperation

The Client agrees to provide Pursuit Pathways with timely access to facilities, personnel, documents, and other resources reasonably necessary for Pursuit Pathways to perform the services. Delays caused by the Client's failure to provide required access or information may result in adjusted timelines and additional fees.

## 4. Onsite Assessments

### 4.1 Nature of Assessments

Pursuit Pathways' onsite facility threat assessments are structured evaluations conducted by trained safety professionals using a proprietary assessment framework aligned with OSHA, CISA, NFPA 3000, and CPTED principles. Assessment reports identify potential vulnerabilities, assign risk scores, and recommend corrective actions.

### 4.2 Scope Limitations

Assessment reports reflect conditions observed at the time of the assessment and are based on information made available to the assessor. Pursuit Pathways does not warrant that assessments will identify every risk or vulnerability present at a facility. The Client is responsible for implementing recommended corrective actions and for maintaining ongoing safety programs.

### 4.3 Access and Safety

The Client is responsible for ensuring that Pursuit Pathways personnel can safely access all areas of the facility to be assessed. The Client must inform Pursuit Pathways of any known hazards, restricted areas, or special access requirements before the assessment date.

## 5. Training Services

### 5.1 In-Person Training

Pursuit Pathways will deliver in-person safety training programs as described in the applicable SOW. Training content is developed by Pursuit Pathways and is based on recognised safety frameworks including ACTD (Assess, Commit, Take Action, Debrief), FEMA ICS/NIMS, and OSHA workplace violence prevention guidelines.

### 5.2 eLearning Programs

Pursuit Pathways may provide access to eLearning modules through the Platform or through a designated learning management system. Access to eLearning content is subject to the terms of the applicable SOW and, where the Platform is used, the Platform Terms of Service.

### 5.3 Train-the-Trainer Programs

Under a Train-the-Trainer SOW, Pursuit Pathways will train designated Client personnel ("**Certified Trainers**") to deliver specified safety training content within the Client's organisation. Upon successful completion of the program, Certified Trainers receive a certification valid for the period specified in the SOW.

The following conditions apply to Train-the-Trainer engagements:

- Certified Trainers may deliver the licensed training content only to employees of the Client organisation and only for internal, non-commercial purposes.
- Certified Trainers may not sublicense, resell, or redistribute training content to third parties.
- The Client is responsible for ensuring that Certified Trainers maintain the quality and accuracy of training delivery.
- Pursuit Pathways retains all intellectual property rights in the training content, including any materials provided to Certified Trainers.
- Certification must be renewed as specified in the SOW; Pursuit Pathways reserves the right to revoke certification for misuse of training materials.

## 6. Fees and Payment

### 6.1 Fees

Fees for Professional Services are specified in the applicable SOW. Year 1 engagements are typically custom-quoted based on the scope of services, facility size, number of participants, and other relevant factors.

### 6.2 Payment Terms

Unless otherwise specified in the SOW, invoices are due within thirty (30) days of the invoice date. Pursuit Pathways accepts payment by credit card (processed by Stripe, Inc.) and by direct bank transfer (ACH or wire). Late payments accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month (or the maximum rate permitted by law, if lower) from the due date until paid.

### 6.3 Expenses

Unless otherwise specified in the SOW, the Client will reimburse Pursuit Pathways for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the services, including travel, lodging, and materials. Expenses will be invoiced with supporting documentation.

### 6.4 Cancellation and Rescheduling

Cancellations or rescheduling requests received fewer than five (5) business days before a scheduled onsite engagement or training session may be subject to a cancellation fee as specified in the SOW. Pursuit Pathways will use commercially reasonable efforts to reschedule cancelled engagements.

Each party will protect the other's Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care. This obligation does not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party; (b) was rightfully known before disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of the Confidential Information; or (d) must be disclosed by law or court order, with prompt written notice to the disclosing party.

Assessment reports and findings are considered Confidential Information of the Client. Pursuit Pathways will not disclose the contents of Client assessment reports to third parties without the Client's prior written consent, except as required by law.

## 12. Term and Termination

### 12.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the date of execution and continues until all active SOWs have been completed, unless earlier terminated as provided herein.

### 12.2 Termination for Cause

Either party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of written notice.

### 12.3 Termination for Convenience

Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon thirty (30) days' written notice, provided that any active SOW will continue until its completion unless both parties agree otherwise. ### 12.4 Survival

Sections 7, 8, 9.2, 10, 11, and 13 survive termination of this Agreement.
